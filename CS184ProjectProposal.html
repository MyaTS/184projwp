Real-Time Graphics: Shaders

Summary:  This project involves creating a new collection of GLSL shaders at three levels of difficulty, distinct from those previously developed. The project emphasizes the vast potential of shader programming, illustrating how intricate visuals can be generated from simple geometric primitives.

Team members: Nicolas Vega, Tony Li, Arthur Pommersheim, Mya Thanegi Soe

Problem Description

Our team is interested in real-time graphics and learning the OpenGL Shading Language. Instead of starting with a large Shader right off the bat, our team plans to develop 3 shaders in increasing order of difficulty.

Our first shader will generate the Mandelbrot set.

Our second shader will generate a fractal shader creating a visually captivating Mandelbulb set renderer that dynamically zooms and explores different regions of the fractal. By utilizing iterative mathematical functions within the shader, the fractal's infinite complexity and self-similarity at various scales can be showcased, with colors assigned based on the iteration count to highlight the fractal's intricate structure.

Our third stretch shader will be some sort of space scene or flux.

If we have time, our team would also be interested in exploring Vulkan.

Goals and Deliverables

A collection of 3-4 shaders to apply in real-time to create fractals.
